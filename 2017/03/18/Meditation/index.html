Preface 序言

Meditation is a practice where an individual trains the mind or induces a mode of consciousness, either to realize some benefit or for the mind to simply acknowledge its content without becoming identified with that content, or as an end in itself.

冥想是一种针对个人精神或者意识进行的修炼，人们希冀于通过冥想来获得某些精神上的益处，或者仅仅简单地用于感知意识本身。

The term meditation refers to a broad variety of practices that includes techniques designed to promote relaxation, build internal energy or life force and develop compassion, love, patience, generosity, and forgiveness. A particularly ambitious form of meditation aims at effortlessly sustained single-pointed concentration meant to enable its practitioner to enjoy an indestructible sense of well-being while engaging in any life activity.

冥想可以指代各种各样的实践，包括旨在促进精神放松，建立内部能量或生命力，发展爱心、同情、慷慨、耐性和宽恕的方法。尤其雄心的，人们希望持续而轻松地专注于一个点，以便在从事任何生活活动时，都能拥有一种牢不可破的舒适感。

The word meditation carries different meanings in different contexts. Meditation has been practiced since antiquity as a component of numerous religious traditions and beliefs. Meditation often involves an internal effort to self-regulate the mind in some way. Meditation is often used to clear the mind and ease many health concerns, such as high blood pressure, depression, and anxiety. It may be done sitting, or in an active way—for instance, Buddhist monks involve awareness in their day-to-day activities as a form of mind-training. Prayer beads or other ritual objects are commonly used during meditation in order to keep track of or remind the practitioner about some aspect of that training.

冥想一词在不同的语境中具有不同的含义。冥想从古代开始就是很多宗教传统和信仰的一部分，冥想经常通过某种内部的方式来对自身意念进行调节控制，冥想也经常通过清静内心来缓解诸多健康问题，比如高血压、抑郁和焦虑等。它可以通过打坐或者其他积极的方式进行，比如，佛教僧侣们经常潜心于他们的日常修行来加强对心性的训练。为了保持训练者对意念的跟踪或在某些方面给出提醒，冥想中经常会用到念珠或其他仪式物品。

Meditation may involve generating an emotional state for the purpose of analyzing that state—such as anger, hatred, etc.—or cultivating a particular mental response to various phenomena, such as compassion. The term “meditation” can refer to the state itself, as well as to practices or techniques employed to cultivate the state. Meditation may also involve repeating a mantra and closing the eyes. The mantra is chosen based on its suitability to the individual meditator. Meditation has a calming effect and directs awareness inward until pure awareness is achieved, described as “being awake inside without being aware of anything except awareness itself.” In brief, there are dozens of specific styles of meditation practice, and many different types of activity commonly referred to as meditative practices.

人们在冥想过程中，可能想起一种情绪状态，而冥想的目的在于分析该情态本身，例如愤怒、仇恨等，冥想也会培养对各种现象（例如同情）的特定心理反应。“冥想”这个词可以指这种状态本身，也可以指用来培养冥想状态的实践或方法。冥想可能会通过闭上眼睛和重复一段咒语来进行，其咒语的选择基于该语句对于冥想者的适用性。冥想有镇静心神的效果，直指意识本身并最终达到纯粹的意识，这是在清醒的情况下，内心除了意识再无其他任何杂念的状态。此外冥想的训练形式和风格也是多种多样的。

Etymology 词源

The English meditation is derived from the Latin meditatio, from a verb meditari, meaning “to think, contemplate, devise, ponder”.

英语中的“Meditation”起源于拉丁语中的“Meditatio”，从动词“Meditari”演变而来，意思是“思索；沉思；想出；反思”。

In the Old Testament, hāgâ (Hebrew: הגה) means to sigh or murmur, and also, to meditate. When the Hebrew Bible was translated into Greek, hāgâ became the Greek melete. The Latin Bible then translated hāgâ/melete into meditatio. The use of the term meditatio as part of a formal, stepwise process of meditation goes back to the 12th-century monk Guigo II.

在旧约圣经中，hāgâ（希伯来语：הגה）是指叹息或低语，也指冥想。当希伯来语圣经被翻译成希腊语时，“hāgâ”变成了希腊语的“melete”。再之后拉丁语圣经把“hāgâ”、“melete”翻译成了“meditatio”。而“meditatio”逐步转变为正式的“meditation”可以追溯到12世纪的修道士盖高二世。

The Tibetan word for meditation “Gom” means “to become familiar with” and has the strong implication of training the mind to be familiar with states that are beneficial: concentration, compassion, correct understanding, patience, humility, perseverance, etc.

藏语中描述冥想的词语“Gom”意思是“熟悉”，另一层强烈含义是指训练心性对有益情态的感知，比如对专注力、同情心、正确认识、耐性、谦逊和毅力等的感知。

Apart from its historical usage, the term meditation was introduced as a translation for Eastern spiritual practices, referred to as dhyāna in Buddhism and in Hinduism, which comes from the Sanskrit root dhyai, meaning to contemplate or meditate. The term “meditation” in English may also refer to practices from Islamic Sufism, or other traditions such as Jewish Kabbalah and Christian Hesychasm.

除了它的历史用法，“冥想”也会在介绍东方的精神训练时作为译词，在佛教和印度教中称为“dhyāna”，源自梵语词根“dhyai”，意为“沉思”或“反省”。在英语中，“冥想”一词也会涉及到伊斯兰教苏菲主义、犹太教神秘主义和基督教静修主义的传统修炼。

An edited book about “meditation” published in 2003, for example, included chapter contributions by authors describing Hindu, Buddhist, Taoist, Jewish, Christian, and Islamic traditions. Scholars have noted that “the term ‘meditation’ as it has entered contemporary usage” is parallel to the term “contemplation” in Christianity, but in many cases, practices similar to modern forms of meditation were simply called ‘prayer’. Christian, Judaic, and Islamic forms of meditation are typically devotional, scriptural or thematic, while Asian forms of meditation are often more purely technical.

2003年出版的一本关于“冥想”的书籍，包含了对印度教、佛教、道教、犹太教、基督教和伊斯兰教传统的介绍。学者们注意到，表示冥想的“Meditation”一词与基督教中现代的“Contemplation”在用法上是对等的，但在许多情况下，之前很多和现代冥想类似的行为被简单描述为“祈祷（Prayer）”。基督教、犹太教、伊斯兰教中的冥想通常是祈祷的、经文的和主旋律的，而亚洲的冥想形式通常是纯粹技术性的。

History 历史

Main article: History of meditation

主条目：冥想的历史

The history of meditation is intimately bound up with the religious context within which it was practiced.[clarification needed] Some authors have even suggested the hypothesis that the emergence of the capacity for focused attention, an element of many methods of meditation, may have contributed to the latest phases of human biological evolution. Some of the earliest references to meditation are found in the Hindu Vedas of Nepal and India. Wilson translates the most famous Vedic mantra ‘Gayatri’ thus : “We meditate on that desirable light of the divine Savitri, who influences our pious rites” (Rigveda: Mandala-3, Sukta-62, Rcha-10). Around the 6th to 5th centuries BCE, other forms of meditation developed via Confucianism and Taoism in China as well as Hinduism, Jainism, and early Buddhism in Nepal and India.

冥想的历史与它在宗教中的实践密切相关。一些作者甚至提出了这样的假设：集中注意力能力的出现以及许多冥想方法中的成分，已经对人类生物进化的最新阶段做出了贡献。一些最早提到冥想的著作出现在印度和尼泊尔的吠陀经上。威尔逊翻译的最著名的吠陀经文伽亚帖是这样写的：

We meditate on that desirable light of the divine Savitri, who influences our pious rites.
我们想念那造就了我们虔信仪式的神圣萨维奇的理想之光！

在公元前6-5世纪，中国的儒家和道家，尼泊尔与印度的印度教、耆那教和早期佛教也发展出了一些其他的冥想形式。

In the west, by 20 BCE Philo of Alexandria had written on some form of “spiritual exercises” involving attention (prosoche) and concentration and by the 3rd century Plotinus had developed meditative techniques.

在西方，公元前20年，亚历山大哲学家斐罗记载了一些关于“精神训练”的形式，包含关注自己和集中注意力的内容，之后公元3世纪普罗提诺发展了冥想的方法。

The Pāli Canon, which dates to 1st century BCE considers Indian Buddhist meditation as a step towards liberation. By the time Buddhism was spreading in China, the Vimalakirti Sutra which dates to 100 CE included a number of passages on meditation, clearly pointing to Zen (known as Chan in China, Thiền in Vietnam, and Seon in Korea). The Silk Road transmission of Buddhism introduced meditation to other Asian countries, and in 653 the first meditation hall was opened in Singapore. Returning from China around 1227, Dōgen wrote the instructions for zazen.

公元前1世纪的巴利文大藏经认为印度佛教冥想是冥想历史发展中解放性的一步。当佛教在中国传播的时候，历史可追溯到公元100年的维摩诘经记载了一些关于冥想的段落，它们明确地指向了禅宗（越南称为Thiền，韩国称为Seon）。丝绸之路上佛教的传播又将冥想传入到其他亚洲国家，在653年，第一座冥想的禅堂在新加坡建立。1227年日本的永平道元禅师从中国回国后，记述了坐禅的方法。

The Islamic practice of Dhikr had involved the repetition of the 99 Names of God since the 8th or 9th century. By the 12th century, the practice of Sufism included specific meditative techniques, and its followers practiced breathing controls and the repetition of holy words. Interactions with Indians, Nepalese or the Sufis may have influenced the Eastern Christian meditation approach to hesychasm, but this can not be proved. Between the 10th and 14th centuries, hesychasm was developed, particularly on Mount Athos in Greece, and involves the repetition of the Jesus prayer.

公元8-9世纪，伊斯兰教徒通过复述99个真主名字的赞念，也是冥想的一种。12世纪，苏菲教派的信奉者通过控制呼吸和复述赞圣词来进行冥想。与印度人、尼泊尔人和苏菲主义者的交流可能影响了东方基督教的冥想方式，使其接近于静修主义，但这无法被证实。10-14世纪，东正教静修派发展起来，尤其是在希腊的阿索斯山上，它冥想实践也包含了对耶稣祷告的复述。

Western Christian meditation contrasts with most other approaches in that it does not involve the repetition of any phrase or action and requires no specific posture. Western Christian meditation progressed from the 6th century practice of Bible reading among Benedictine monks called Lectio Divina, i.e. divine reading. Its four formal steps as a “ladder” were defined by the monk Guigo II in the 12th century with the Latin terms lectio, meditatio, oratio, and contemplatio (i.e. read, ponder, pray, contemplate). Western Christian meditation was further developed by saints such as Ignatius of Loyola and Teresa of Avila in the 16th century.

西方基督教的冥想方式与其他大多数方式形成对比，它不涉及语句和动作的重复，对姿势也没有具体要求。西方基督教的冥想从6世纪本笃会修道士的圣经诵读，一种神圣的阅读方式发展而来。12世纪，修道士盖高二世定义了圣经诵读的四个正式的梯级步骤，拉丁文分别为lectio、meditatio、oratio和contemplatio，意即阅读、回想、祈祷和沉思。西方基督教的冥想在16世纪由圣徒们进一步发展，如罗耀拉的圣依纳爵、亚维拉的圣女德肋撒等。

Secular forms of meditation were introduced in India in the 1950s as a Westernized form of Hindu meditative techniques and arrived in the United States and Europe in the 1960s. Rather than focusing on spiritual growth, secular meditation emphasizes stress reduction, relaxation and self-improvement. Both spiritual and secular forms of meditation have been subjects of scientific analyses. Research on meditation began in 1931, with scientific research increasing dramatically during the 1970s and 1980s. Since the beginning of the ‘70s more than a thousand studies of meditation in English-language have been reported. However, after 60 years of scientific study, the exact mechanism at work in meditation remains unclear.

现俗世的冥想，在20世纪50年代以印度教冥想的一种西方化形式传入印度，60年代传播到了美国和欧洲。它不是专注于精神成长的，而是强调减轻压力，放松和自我完善。宗教和世俗的冥想形式都是科学分析的课题。对冥想的科学研究始于1931年，在20世纪70到80年代急剧增加。20世纪70年代以来，有超过1000多篇关于冥想的英语论文发表出来。然而，经过60多年的科学研究，冥想作用的确切机制仍不清楚。
